Interview with Permanent Employees, Career Counsellor and Gig Workers

Key Findings from Our Interviews (Permanent Employees)

Why They Choose Permanent Employment

  • Lack of experience for independent work
  • Not mentally prepared for business risks
  • Prefer working under supervision to gain experience
  • Value skill development opportunities

Benefits They Value

  • Fixed salary for family support
  • Paid leave benefits
  • Health insurance coverage
  • Job security and stability

Work Environment Impact

Employees shared that working under supervision creates productive pressure that helps them maintain order and complete work on time. The structured environment motivates them and provides clear expectations, which they feel is lacking in freelance or gig work where self-discipline is crucial.

Financial Security Concerns

A significant factor influencing their preference for permanent jobs is the guaranteed fixed salary. They expressed concerns about the unpredictable income in gig work or freelancing, where profit and loss responsibility lies entirely with the individual. The assurance of stable income allows them to confidently support themselves and their families.

Cultural and Educational Factors

Interviewees mentioned that their mindset was developed from early on to pursue "proper jobs" (traditional employment). They noted a lack of awareness and knowledge about alternative work arrangements like freelancing or entrepreneurship during their educational years, which influenced their career choices.

Insights from Global Perspectives

INDRIVE COUNTRY MANAGER (Pakistan)

Mr. Awais Saeed of InDrive emphasized that gig work and traditional jobs are fundamentally different and not directly comparable. People in Pakistan often choose gig work due to a lack of traditional opportunities and income flexibility. He noted that gig workers do not receive benefits like healthcare or job security, and their service is highly dependent on customer feedback, which can lead to termination even on a single complaint. CAREER COUNSELLOR (Pakistan)

Based on 20 years of experience, the counsellor noted that there has never been a fixed or guaranteed pattern of employment and that both permanent and gig work can be unstable. Many choose gig work for freedom and convenience over strict office hours, but this freedom comes with a lack of security, describing employment as "cutthroat." While office jobs bring discipline, deadlines, and socialization, he noted that most private jobs in Pakistan do not offer pensions or provident funds. He stressed that permanent employment is still more valued in society for stable pay and a sense of job security. He emphasized that communication, courtesy, manners, and IT skills are basic requirements for success in any career path.

GLOBAL EMPLOYEE (Canada)

The employee currently based in Canada highlighted the high cost of living in cities like Toronto and Vancouver, where gig work income alone makes survival difficult, especially for those with families. She noted that large companies like General Motors prioritize full-time employees and do not typically hire gig workers, suggesting that permanent jobs are still considered more stable in Canada. Many people have to combine part-time or gig work with full-time jobs to manage expenses. She concluded that while freelancing might work in some European countries, in major Canadian cities, full-time employment is generally essential for survival** unless one possesses exceptional skills.
